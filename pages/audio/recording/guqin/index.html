    • I began recording things during the pandemic after I started micing up my guqin for online lessons with a mic I borrowed from my flatmate. I didn't know anything about best practices while recording acoustic instruments or anything about EQing and post-production. The first thing I noticed was how much more accurately I could hear my slide notes. The second thing I noticed was how loud and unique the sounds of my shared apartment were.

    My primary tool is my Sony PCM-A10 recorder, but I often fall back on my phone (previously a Google Pixel 3, now a Samsung Galaxy S21 FE). I also use a pair of LOM mikroUÅ¡is, mostly for recording inside.

    I embraced the honesty and imperfections of my early recordings in their entirety, my amateur playing, the sounds of my flatmates in the apartment -- talking on the phone, cooking in the kitchen -- as well as the sounds of the neighborhood outside, including the beautiful prayer call of the mosque across the street. Eventually, I began to capture more recordings outside to capture the unique ambiences of the surrounding areas.
