    Our "source" index (as opposed to topic, beat, or byline indexing) provides a completely new dimension with which to explore storytelling. These new modular components would not only serve as a tool for auditing our sourcing diversity, they would also provide a new way for readers to search through our articles e.g. quotes from prospective homebuyers in the Midwest or comments from sociologists based out of NYC. "Pitched by a team from The Wall Street Journal working in New York and London, the experiment looked at breaking stories down into modules by identifying the people mentioned in different parts of them. By pulling out these blocks, allowing journalists to annotate the data in the content management system and then programmatically organising it, the team demonstrated how their prototype could be used to analyse the appearances of sources in stories in the hope of providing an audit for diversity and even a search tool for those working in the newsroom. The prototype tackled the challenge of gaining scalable and timely insight into sourcing in news. The judges commended the prototype's rapid feedback loop, allowing for more timely analysis and actionable insight rather than annual reports or bespoke analytics projects to tackle the same issues."
